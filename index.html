About Me

I am currently a Research Assistant Professor at the Institute of Space and Earth Information Science, The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK). Prior to joining CUHK, I was a Postdoctoral Scholar at The Pennsylvania State University (2024‚Äì2025). I obtained my Ph.D. in Communication and Transportation Engineering from Southeast University in 2023.

My research focuses on the intersection of Civil Engineering and Artificial Intelligence. I specialize in integrating multi-source monitoring technologies (GPR, FWD) with Deep Learning and Digital Twins to enhance infrastructure resilience [cite: 14-16].

I have been recognized as one of the "World's Top 2% Scientists" by Stanford University & Elsevier (2024, 2025).

Research Interests

My research aims to solve complex engineering challenges through interdisciplinary approaches:

  • Intelligent Infrastructure Inspection: Non-destructive testing using GPR and FWD.
  • AI for Civil Engineering (AI4CE): Computer vision (YOLO, Transformers) for distress detection.
  • Digital Twins: BIM and GIS integration for lifecycle management.
  • Sustainable Materials: Low-carbon technologies and recycled materials.
