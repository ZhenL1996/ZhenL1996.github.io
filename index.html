About Me

I am currently a Research Assistant Professor at the Institute of Space and Earth Information Science, The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK). Prior to joining CUHK, I was a Postdoctoral Scholar at The Pennsylvania State University (2024–2025). I obtained my Ph.D. in Communication and Transportation Engineering from Southeast University in 2023.

My research focuses on the intersection of Civil Engineering and Artificial Intelligence. I specialize in integrating multi-source monitoring technologies (GPR, FWD) with Deep Learning and Digital Twins to enhance infrastructure resilience.

I have been recognized as one of the "World's Top 2% Scientists" by Stanford University & Elsevier (2024, 2025).

Research Interests

My research aims to address critical challenges in transportation infrastructure through interdisciplinary technologies:

  • Intelligent Infrastructure Inspection: Non-destructive testing using Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) and FWD.
  • AI for Civil Engineering (AI4CE): Utilizing Computer Vision (YOLO, Transformers) for automated distress detection.
  • Digital Twins: BIM and GIS integration for lifecycle management of infrastructure.
  • Sustainable Materials: Performance evaluation of recycled asphalt and low-carbon technologies.
